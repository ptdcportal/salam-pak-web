Fall banishes away the hot and humid weather of the summer, and invites a mild atmosphere that you’ll prefer to the harsh winters. These months are part of the transition into the off-season, making it a good time to travel to Pakistan to avoid packed attractions and peak-season prices. Autumn also offers many delicious fruits and festivities for people to enjoy, ensuring that there will seldom be a dull moment to your trip to Pakistan during this mild and pleasant time.

The Sights of Fall

Falling leaves are a hallmark of autumn everywhere, and Pakistan perfectly plays that expected scene during this season. Orange leaves drifting in the air and piling up are common sights in the green regions of Pakistan, along with the best sunsets of all year.

With the mild weather comes mostly clear skies, though the odd shower does create beautiful overcasts in the country. Streams and lakes also continue to sparkle and shine during the fall season, making it a good time to experience Pakistan’s beauty.

Seasonal Foods & Fruits

While many fruits are available throughout the year in Pakistan, many maintain that some of the best-tasting varieties of certain fruits bloom in fall.

Such fruits include bananas and apples. Many of the winter fruits such as pear, grapefruit, and pomegranate also start reaching tables in the country, making this a good time to sink your teeth into some winter treats without the harsher weather. One particular specialty of the autumn season is the sharifa tropical fruit (custard apple) which has a unique taste and flavor seldom available elsewhere.

Traveling in fall

The falls months are a happy medium between the intensity of summer and winter. The snow is not a factor in hindering any travels, and the cooling weather makes for a pleasant experience relative to the scorching summer.

As the season progresses, the cold starts to creep in, so while you won’t need to pack your heaviest jackets (unless traveling to the mountainous north), some warm clothes are still prudent. The fall also marks the end of the summer peak season, making it a good time to come to Pakistan if you don’t want to face heavy crowds.

fall Festivities and Events

A great time to have fun and experience the culture of Pakistan, the fall season hosts many events in the country.

1Lok Festival

A 10-day festival in Islamabad’s Lok Virsa museum that celebrates Pakistani folk culture. Expect to see provincial cultural pavilions, artisans at work, folk songs and dance performances, exotic craft bazaar, cultural nights, folkloric performances and many other activities.

2Defence Day

On the 6th of September, Pakistan celebrates a successful military defence. To honor the day and sacrifice, tanks and other military arsenal are displayed in Islamabad as a part of a parade, which also features air shows with fighter jets. Definitely a stunning display.

3independence Day

The day Pakistan came into being is celebrated on the 14th of August with massive celebrations across the country. Green and white mark the scenery as lights, flags, and paint is used for decorations and fireworks can be heard all day.

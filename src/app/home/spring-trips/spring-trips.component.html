Clear skies, a cool breeze, blossoming flowers, and mild weather are the hallmarks of spring in Pakistan. The intense natural splendour of the country is only magnified further during this season, and that combined with the low precipitation makes this time well suited for an unforgettable trip. Add to the gorgeous sights and pleasant weather many unique foods and festivities that spring brings, and you have a very strong reason to land in Pakistan in the early months of the year.

The Sights of Spring

As the weather cools down, the flowers begin to grow and paint the scenery with beautiful colors. This is especially apparent in the meadows and fields across the country, particularly in the north.

With the clear skies and minimal cloud cover, you’ll be able to enjoy the changing flora even more and your hikes and treks will become more scenic as well! As the glaciers of winter melt away, the most beautiful of streams and waterfalls are woken from frozen slumber and start to flow unhindered, adding to the already-breathtaking scene in the north. If you’re looking to experience natural beauty at its peak, spring might be the perfect time to visit Pakistan.

Seasonal Foods & Fruits

The season of spring brings with it some delicious fruits to Pakistan, which serve as a precursor to the full fruit bonanza that begins in the summer.

The strawberry comes to life exclusively during the spring season, while another berry-like fruit that’s a local hit begins to reach fruit stands: falsa. Falsa, or grewia asiatica, is a small, sweet & sour fruit that’s enjoyed raw, as a juice, and as a street food mixed with special spices to glean a unique flavor. You’ll also be able to sink your teeth into delicious, plump apricots during these months. See, spring is not just about the sights and sounds — it has its unique flavors as well!

Traveling in Spring

Spring clears up several roads and highways of snow and ice, unlocking virtually all of Pakistan, especially many of the must-see northern areas. Short of the odd shower, the weather is also clear and mild, which makes for a painless journey.

Visiting the colder areas of the country like the north and parts of Balochistan may still be chilly, but nearly not as much as during the winter. All in all, spring is an excellent time to travel to and through Pakistan, especially considering that you’ll avoid the peak time of the summer months.

Spring Festivities & Events

A season of many colors, spring is made even more vibrant in Pakistan with the many festivities that come alive during the early year.

1Chilam Jusht

In Khyber Pakhtunkhwa’s Chitral District, dance to the beat to the drums and sound of joyous music as this annual festival of the Kalash people celebrates spring, calls for festive clothing, and hopes to make matches between the participating men and women!

2Nowruz

A celebration to mark the beginning of the new year in the Persian calendar, Nowruz is an important event for the Ismaili and Parsi communities in Pakistan. It’s a time for family, food, and festivities that everyone can enjoy.

3Mela Chiraghan

Mela Chiraghan, or the Festival of Lamps, pays homage to Sufi saint Shah Hussain in his native Lahore, and is one of the biggest festivals in the country. The event features elaborate decor, oil lamps, and mystical dances and music — an authentic Sufi experience.

4National Horse and Cattle Show

Another annual event in Lahore, the Horse and Cattle Show multi-day event with many festivities that invites cattle and horse breeders to compete in animal shows. The most majestic of animals from the country are the chief guests at this festival.

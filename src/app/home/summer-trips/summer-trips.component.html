Summer

The perfect time for both beaches and mountains, summer is the most popular for travel in Pakistan for very good reason.

While things might get hot and humid during the summer, it makes the very best of Pakistan accessible. The warmer weather means you can pack lighter, and it means the frigid yet breathtakingly beautiful north becomes a much friendlier destination. Known for its food, Pakistan’s palate shifts significantly during the summer as many fruits become available and the street foods change to more refreshing and colder options. All this, combined with numerous exciting festivals make the summer months an amazing time to visit Pakistan — and let’s not forget the beaches as well!

The Sights of summer

The bloom of magnificent flowers from spring carries over in the summer months, with some new species adding even more color to where flora is seen.

Since the season brings rain and clouds with it, many days during the summer offer a relaxing, cool atmosphere with an inoffensive overcast and some showers that bring out the absolute best of places like Islamabad. While the shining sun does make things hot and humid at times, it also allows the lakes, rivers, and sea to shimmer brighter, making for quite the spectacle.

Seasonal Foods & Fruits

Summer calls for a fruit fiesta in Pakistan. A large variety of delicious local favorites are harvested during this time, and the incredible quality of fruit can leave one quickly eating more calories than planned.

Juicy watermelons, delectable peaches, addictive black plums (jamun), and of course, perhaps the king of all fruits in Pakistan: mangoes — all of these and more are summer’s gifts. The chaunsa variety of mango is unique to Pakistan and offers an unbelievably fresh and pulpy taste. On the street food side of things, the landscape also shifts with treats like lemon soda, falooda (an embellished ice cream-based dessert), and the ever-refreshing ganay ka ras (sugarcane juice) make their way to hawkers everywhere.

Traveling in summer

Many popular destinations in the north of Pakistan are inaccessible during the winter or late spring due to heavy snow and ice on the roads.

However, summer ensures that no such obstacles will stand in your way, unlocking Pakistan in its entirety for travel. For this reason, and due to summer holidays, internal tourism within the country also reaches a peak, which means you will find all tourist spots and activities accessible during this time, albeit a little crowded. Finally, you can expect long days during the summer, and the added sun time will give you more time to explore the beauty and charm of Pakistan to your heart’s content!

Summer Festivities and Events

The shining sun of summer invites with a vigor and passion within Pakistan that is reflected in many memorable local events.

1Raksha Bandhan

A popular festivity celebrated by Hindus throughout Pakistan to renew their vows to protect and serve their families and loved ones. Taking place in late August, Raksha Bandhan brings many colors with it in decor and smiles.

2Shandur Polo Festival

The highest polo ground in the world at Shandur in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa hosts the Shandur Polo Festival on 7-9th July every year, which is a landmark three-day event in the country. Apart from intense polo, the festival features a camping space and live folk music & dance.

3Kalam Festival

The valley of Swat in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa is famous for this rich and rousing festival which features a number of local sporting events, traditional dance performances, and folk music concerts. Paragliding, jeeping, canoeing are also among the many activities on offer.

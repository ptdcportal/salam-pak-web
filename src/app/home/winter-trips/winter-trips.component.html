While winter brings frigid temperatures and snowy obstacles in the north and far-west of Pakistan, it also brings with it all sorts of snow-related activities. A few areas that are closed off due to snow are more than made up for by how pleasant the coastal cities become — where many would argue is the heart of Pakistan, and not to mention its beaches! Since the colder months are part of the off-season, it’s a great time to travel without running into crowds and on a budget, all while still enjoying the incredible sight and sounds of Pakistan and its many cultural events.

The Sights of Winter

Snow comes in winter time to both the north of Pakistan and to its west in Balochistan. This dramatically changes the experience in both regions, where activities like skiing become available.

With the snow, the mountain peaks are painted a royal white, and the skies are usually clear when snow isn’t falling, making beautiful sunsets still a possibility. Glaciers are also naturally formed during the cold months, adding more variety to the scenery with nature’s very own ice sculptures.

Seasonal Foods & Fruits

The colder months see a new breed of fruits bloom in Pakistan. Among these winter treats are oranges, carrots, pomegranates, sweet potatoes, pears, guavas, and pumpkins.

When it comes to oranges in particular, the keenu variety (kinnow) of mandarins is exclusive to the Punjab region — which lies only in Pakistan and India. Seafood and many varieties of fish, while available all year round, also become more popular in everyday cuisine during the winter. And finally, on the street food side of things, a local version of chicken corn soup and chicken broth are sold as yummy snacks, as are pomegranate seeds with a delicious spice for flavoring.

Traveling in Winter

With heavy snowfall in the north, some remote areas become inaccessible. But those who can withstand the cold will still have many of the north’s stunning sights to look forward to and things like skiing and a host of other snow-related activities.

Moving away from the north, the rest of Pakistan does not see much snow, though many areas still call for a jacket. The coastline area, and Karachi in particular, while uncomfortably hot at times in the summer, are perfectly pleasant in the winter. So if you want the best experience of what Karachi has to offer — the culture, the food, the nightlife, and more — winter might just be the best time to swing by.

Winter Festivities and Events

1Choimus

Bonfires, folk dance, ornate traditional clothing — all are sights to see during the Kalash people’s Choimus Festival in Chitral Valley, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. This is a religious event and prayers are made to one of the gods of the Kalash people, Balmain.

2Eat Festivals

Several festivals around major cities pop up during the winter months. Examples include “Karachi Eat Festival” and “Lahore Eat Festival”. All of these are great occasions to try out an exceptionally wide variety of local cuisine in one place while enjoying concerts & other activities.

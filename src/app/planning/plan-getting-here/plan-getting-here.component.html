Getting to Pakistan

Normally, there are direct flights to Pakistan from several cities around the world. From some countries, you will also have the option of travelling by bus.

By plane

Normally, there are direct flights to Pakistan from the coasts of Asia, Europe, and North America. Jinnah International Airport, located in Karachi, is Pakistan’s largest international and domestic airport. It is the main hub for international traffic flow in and out of Pakistan. However, be prepared to change your flights at some point, in order to reach your final destination in Pakistan. Pakistan International Airlines operates in 23 international destinations in 14 countries across Asia, Europe, and North America as of June 2020.

Other airports with international flights can be found near the cities of Islamabad, Lahore, Peshawar, Multan, Sialkot, Faisalabad, Quetta, Rahim Yar Khan, Turbat, Gwadar and D.G. Khan. All larger cities and towns have airports offering both international and domestic flights.

By bus

You can reach Pakistan via bus from Pakistan’s neighboring countries, China, Afghanistan and Iran, upon availability of travel visas from these countries to Pakistan. Bus routes from India to Pakistan are temporarily suspended. Currently, China and Pakistan are connected only by the Karakoram Highway via a single crossing in the Khunjerab Pass. Whereas Afghanistan is connected to Pakistan via the Khyber Pass which connects Kabul to Peshawar. You can use a bus to reach Taftan, Iran from Quetta, Pakistan.

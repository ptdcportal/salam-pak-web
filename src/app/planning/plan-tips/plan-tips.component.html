Top Travel Tips

Make your experience of visiting Pakistan an amazing and memorable one by being well-prepared, and don’t be shy to ask local tourist offices for their updated tips and advice. Here is your quick guide to useful information.

Budget travel

Travel in Pakistan is economically quite feasible, as there are lots of things you can see and do here without messing up your holiday budget.

1. Book as early as possible

That way you have a far better chance of securing the most affordable tickets for public transportation such as flights, trains and busses – and also the cheapest accommodation.

2. Travel in the off-season

This is a neat way to obtain cheaper accommodation and transportation, and you won’t have to share the popular attractions with quite as many others. You can also save money if you travel like a local, using public transport.

3. Cheap accommodation

Pakistan has a great variety of camping sites with space for people to enjoy the breathtaking scenery with their families and friends. They are also meant for people who love solitude and are looking for a few quiet, uninterrupted days to themselves. It is a good idea to book a slot in advance if you are travelling in the high season. You can set up your camping gear in the camping sites. If you prefer, you can choose to lodge at a hotel/ guest house near your campsite, ranging from the very basic to the highly luxurious type. You may find lodging at such guesthouses at a reasonable rate. However, if you wish to truly enjoy and gain the true experience of Pakistan’s northern areas, we would recommend you to camp out in the open space.

4. Travel together

Plan your trip to Pakistan together with your extended family or a group of good friends. That way, you can rent a large guesthouse, flat or house, and use this as a base. This is an economical solution that can cut costs significantly.

5. Buy food locally

Having the opportunity to cook for yourself, at least for certain parts of your trip, will work wonders on your budget. You can cook yourself a delicious picnic or dinner from local delicacies such as Chicken or Beef Karahi, Biryani or Halwa Puri without putting too much strain on your budget. You can even enjoy street food for your breakfast, lunch, or dinner from local restaurants near your camping site, which should be perfectly economical.

6. Fresh drinks

A proper water bottle that keeps its contents cold is a good investment, and you can also fill it with crystal clear, clean, fresh water directly from natural springs at various locations. You may enjoy Lassi or buttermilk, a delightful Punjabi drink for breakfast at local restaurants.

Calling home

Dial “00” followed by your two or three digit country code, the area code and phone number. On most mobile phones, using “+” instead of “00” will also work. If you are calling on a mobile phone, buying a local pre-paid SIM card may be the cheapest option. Commonly used mobile networks in Pakistan are Ufone, Mobilink, Zong, and Telenor

Currency

Pakistani currency is the Pakistani Rupee (PKR).

Disability

Pakistan airline employees are trained to assist passengers with certain disabilities and offer them a wide variety of special assistance. Such passengers with special needs will be provided with assistance in moving to and from their seats, help in preparation for meals, and assistance in use of an inflight wheelchair to and from the lavatory door.

Driving

Almost all the cities of Pakistan are now readily accessible by the motorways, and the internal parts of the cities are also connected by highways. The time taken to reach a particular destination depends on the traffic and weather conditions, although traffic on the motorways rarely reaches a high point and they are mostly clear to drivers to relax and drive smoothly. However, weather conditions such as rain and fog may sometimes impede the flow of traffic on motorways and highways, which may affect the destination time. However, if you are aware about the oncoming weather conditions and leave for your journey on a timely basis, you should be able to reach your destination at your planned time. You will be able to enjoy several scenic views on your way to your destination if you take the motorway, such as lakes, rivers and mountains. You may stop on the way at a rest area, which may have filling stations as well so you may refuel your car and recharge yourself with a meal for your remaining journey.

Customs and regulations

You are permitted to bring the following articles free of customs and excise duty into Pakistan:

  • Gift articles and/ or souvenirs in a reasonable number and quantity. For the first visit in one calendar year, the value for imported gifts may not be more than PKR 2000, for the second visit, the value may not be more than PKR 1000, and for the third or subsequent visits, imported gifts are not duty free.
  • 200 cigarettes or 50 cigars or 1/2 kilogram of tobacco;
  • 1/4 liter of eau de toilette and perfume, of which not more than 1/8th liters may be perfume.

It is prohibited to bring the following items into Pakistan with certain exceptions as mentioned:

  • Alcoholic beverages, both for residents and non-residents regardless of nationality.
  • Matches, fruits, plants and plant material (including cut flowers) whether live or dead unless supported by a Phytosanitary (Health) certificate from the country of origin and an import permit from the Pakistan Ministry of Agriculture .
  • Banknotes in denominations of 50 and 100 rupees or more.

The following products are restricted:

  • Import permit and Arms licence for weapons required.
  • Currency import regulations: Local currency (Pakistani Rupee), PKR 100 in denominations of 10 rupees or less, and in combinations of personal quota.
  • Pets and animals must be supported by a veterinarian certificate. Pets may be included in passenger’s checked baggage, as cargo, or in the cabin.

Electricity

The standard requirement for electrical devices in Pakistan is 230 V and 50 Hz, so do make sure to bring the appropriate travel adaptor with you when you travel to Pakistan.

Internet access

The internet access and speed is usually quite good in most towns and cities in Pakistan, however several places may not have internet access available, in which case it would be better to get a data package for whichever mobile service you are using, or get a roaming data package online or through the respective mobile helpline. Mobile data coverage may be patchy in sparsely populated areas. Most hotels have free Internet access in the rooms, however it’s best to ask the hotel before you book. Many buses and trains offer free onboard Wi-Fi, so you’ll be able to connect easily.

Public Holidays

Pakistani’s main public holidays include Independence Day (14 August), Defence Day (6 September), Pakistan Day (23 March), Iqbal Day (9 November), Labour Day (1 May), Quaid e Azam Day (25 December), Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Azha, and Youm-e-Ashura. Several main shops and markets remain open during these holidays, however the smaller shops usually remain closed, or close earlier.

Smoking policies

Indoor smoking is prohibited in most public places in Pakistan, including hotels, restaurants, and all other establishments where food and beverages are served. There are several hotels which have smoking lounges available for guests who would like to smoke. E-cigarettes which contain nicotine are permitted, as well as vaping, but not in public places like restaurants and offices.

Time zone

Pakistan uses one time zone, which is Pakistan Standard Time (PKT). This is UTC+05:00, meaning that it is five hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time.

Visa requirements and passports

Most foreign nationals require a visit visa to travel to Pakistan, as well as a passport which is valid for at least 6 months from the intended date of arrival in Pakistan. The type of visa, application procedures and visa requirements will vary according to the traveller’s nationality, the length of the planned stay in the country and the purpose of the visit.

Currently, there are 5 countries whose citizens can travel to Pakistan visa-free for short stays: all other foreign visitors must either receive an online visa for Pakistan through an electronic application (eVisa), a visa on arrival, or a visa from a Pakistani embassy, depending upon their nationality. You must apply for a Pakistani visa in your country of origin in most cases, however you are permitted to apply for a third country if you are legal permanent residents of that country. The majority of foreign citizens can apply for a eVisa for Pakistan through an electronic application, with a few nationalities as an exception.

An eVisa authorization can be approved for a permitted stay of between 14 to 90 days, and as either a single or multiple entry permit, depending on the destination. They usually allow travel for either tourism, transit, or business reasons.
